# Warranty Policy — Home Appliances Division

This policy defines the manufacturer warranty terms, exclusions, and the process for handling functional faults reported during the warranty period.

## §1 — Coverage Duration

1.1 Standard warranty coverage is **12 months** from the date of delivery for all parts and labour, unless a different period is printed on the warranty card supplied with the unit.

1.2 Extended warranties, if purchased, run consecutively after the standard warranty and cover the same defects unless otherwise noted on the extended-warranty certificate.

1.3 Warranty status is determined from the invoice date and the delivery date. If the warranty card has been mutilated or the serial number is illegible, the warranty is treated as void until the customer provides a verifiable proof of purchase.

## §2 — Exclusions

2.1 The following are excluded from warranty coverage:
   (a) damage caused by misuse, neglect, accident, or unauthorised modification,
   (b) cosmetic wear and tear (minor scratches, fading, fabric wear), including damage that occurs after the return window has expired,
   (c) consumable parts (filters, bulbs, gaskets, belts) beyond their normal service life,
   (d) damage caused by use of non-approved accessories or consumables,
   (e) damage caused by installation by an unauthorised installer.

2.2 Exclusions are determined on a case-by-case basis. Where evidence is inconclusive, the case shall be escalated to the regional service manager.

## §3 — Functional Fault Process

3.1 A "functional fault" is defined as any defect that prevents the product from performing its intended function — for example, refrigerator not cooling, washer not spinning, oven not heating, AC unit not turning on. Cosmetic or packaging damage is **not** a functional fault and is governed by the Return Policy.

3.2 When a customer reports a functional fault, the customer service agent shall:
   (a) attempt **remote troubleshooting** first for any software, app, or interface issue and for any electrical complaint where the symptom is described as intermittent;
   (b) dispatch an **authorised technician** for any mechanical, thermal, or persistent electrical complaint;
   (c) NEVER approve a direct replacement based solely on photographic evidence for a functional fault — photos cannot prove or rule out a functional defect.

3.3 If the technician confirms the fault and it is covered under §1 and not excluded under §2, the standard remedy is repair. Replacement is authorised only if the technician documents that repair is not economically viable or that the same defect has recurred after two prior repairs.

3.4 The full functional-fault chain (complaint → remote troubleshoot → technician → repair-or-replace) shall be recorded in the case audit log.
