# UAE Consumer Protection Policy — Home Appliances

This policy incorporates obligations under UAE Federal Law No. 15 of 2020 on Consumer Protection and its Executive Regulations, as applied to the home appliances retail division operating within the UAE.

## §1 — Consumer Rights Under UAE Federal Law No. 15 of 2020

1.1 Every consumer has the right to obtain a product that conforms to the specifications, standards, and quality declared at the time of sale, as stipulated under Article 5 of Federal Law No. 15/2020.

1.2 The consumer has the right to a full refund or replacement if the product does not conform to the advertised specifications, provided the non-conformance is reported within the statutory return window and is not caused by consumer misuse.

1.3 The seller is prohibited from selling products that do not comply with UAE ESMA (Emirates Authority for Standardisation and Metrology) technical regulations or Gulf Standardisation Organisation (GSO) standards applicable to home appliances.

1.4 All home appliances sold in the UAE must carry a valid ESMA conformity mark (UAE.S). Any claim for defects that would have been detectable at manufacture shall be treated as the seller's liability under Article 8 of Federal Law No. 15/2020.

## §2 — Statutory Return and Refund Entitlement

2.1 Under the UAE Consumer Protection Executive Regulations (Cabinet Resolution No. 66 of 2023), the consumer is entitled to return any product within **15 calendar days** of delivery if the product:
   (a) does not match its description or specification as advertised,
   (b) is found to be defective on arrival (DOA) with documentation,
   (c) is delivered incomplete (missing accessories, parts, or documentation listed on the invoice).

2.2 For products returned under §2.1(a) or §2.1(b), the seller must process the return or replacement within **7 working days** of accepting the return request.

2.3 Where the seller disputes the defect claim, an independent technical assessment must be offered to the consumer within 15 calendar days. The seller bears the cost of such assessment if the assessment confirms the defect.

2.4 Returns initiated due to **change of mind** (not defective) are not mandated under UAE law. Such returns are governed solely by the company's voluntary return policy (see Return Policy §1).

## §3 — Warranty Obligations Under UAE Law

3.1 In accordance with Article 10 of Federal Law No. 15/2020, every product sold in the UAE must be accompanied by a minimum warranty period as specified by the relevant technical regulations. For home appliances, the minimum warranty is **12 months** for parts and **24 months** for the compressor/motor (for applicable categories).

3.2 The warranty must be honoured by the authorised service network in the UAE regardless of where within the UAE the product was purchased, provided the product was purchased through an authorised distributor.

3.3 The seller cannot void a warranty on the grounds that the consumer did not register the product, unless product registration was an explicit and clearly communicated pre-condition stated on the warranty card at time of sale.

3.4 Warranty does not cover consumables (filters, bulbs, belts, hoses) unless the failure of the consumable is caused by a manufacturing defect in the primary unit.

## §4 — Consumer Complaints and Escalation Rights

4.1 If the consumer's complaint is not resolved within the statutory period, the consumer has the right to escalate the complaint to the Ministry of Economy's Consumer Protection Department (CPD) through the Consumer Protection portal or by calling 600 522225.

4.2 The company shall maintain a documented case trail for every consumer complaint, including the complaint date, evidence submitted, assessment outcome, and final resolution. This record must be retained for a minimum of **5 years** as required under Article 15 of the Executive Regulations.

4.3 Failure to resolve a valid consumer complaint within the statutory timeframe may result in administrative penalties under Article 21 of Federal Law No. 15/2020, including fines of up to AED 2,000,000 for repeat violations.

## §5 — Product Safety Recalls

5.1 If any home appliance model sold by the company is subject to a UAE market recall order issued by ESMA or the Ministry of Economy, the customer must be proactively notified within 48 hours of the company receiving notice of the recall.

5.2 For recall-affected products, the consumer is entitled to a full replacement with a conforming unit at no charge, irrespective of the return window or warranty period.

5.3 Customer service staff must escalate any complaint involving a recalled model to the Regional Service Manager and the Compliance Team immediately.

## §6 — E-Commerce Returns (UAE Federal E-Commerce Law)

6.1 For products purchased through the company's online channels, the consumer has an additional **7 calendar days right of withdrawal** from the date of delivery (regardless of condition) in accordance with UAE Cabinet Resolution No. 21 of 2023 (E-Commerce Executive Regulations).

6.2 The right of withdrawal under §6.1 does not apply to:
   (a) products that have been installed, connected, or configured (rendering them non-resaleable in an unmodified state),
   (b) products with the manufacturer seal broken when the defect is not related to the product's specifications,
   (c) perishable or hygiene-sensitive consumables supplied with the product.

6.3 For valid e-commerce withdrawals, the company shall arrange reverse logistics at no cost to the consumer and issue a refund within 5 working days of receiving the returned product.
